I am starting a PhD in Computer Science this Spring at CU Boulder, working under the supervision of Katharina Kann in the NALA lab.
Natural Language Processing
I work in NLP; at the intersection of language and computer science, with applications that support such technologies as automatic translation, speech to text transcription, and the information extraction. I am especially interested in language technologies geared towards languages that are typically underrepresented in NLP.
Linguistics
The languages spoken by humans have latent structures that we rely on in order to understand each other, however, it is incredibly difficult to identify what those structures are. I have a theoretical background in linguistics, which deals with such topics as categorizing the typological features of languages universally, the study of sound structures, and the nuances of expressing meaning.
Software Development
